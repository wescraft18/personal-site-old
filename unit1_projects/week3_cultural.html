An abstract random thinkers...

Joey S. - 5/30/14

Abstract random thinkers have a hard time concentrating on one thing at a time. This has been my biggest hurdle during my time at DBC thus far. I see 9 challenges and I want to do ALL of them at once. This might be an okay thing to try, but I just dont think it helps kme in the long run.

I'm not entirely sure this is related to my learning style so much as there is a ridiculous amount of outside distractions fighting for my attention on a daily basis. I try and balance, but it sure is difficult.

My plan to get on track is to use the time management methods we learned about in week two of DBC phase 0. I want to set small time restrictions on what I do intermixed with breaks to help me keep focus without drifting into unncessary items.

My learning style says I have a hard time working in restrictive enviornments and with explaining things. This is becomes a problem in group settings as my opinion becomes difficult to justify to my group members. I haven't really run into these things yet but have noticed that I tend to take a back seat in a lot of the pairing sessions.

I want to expand my horizons and learn to take criticism better while being able to express my views and take a confident stance in them. I am on the process of learning and growing.
